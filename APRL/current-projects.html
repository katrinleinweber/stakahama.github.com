Available positions for Postdoc/Ph.D. candidates:

  • Measurement and characterization of aerosol composition and properties. A candidate for this position should have an interest in developing, testing, and deploying instrument(s) for measurement of aerosol composition and physical/chemical properties. Heavy emphasis will be placed on spectroscopy and microscopy, with LabView software integration and computer programming for data analysis. The position will involve 1) laboratory experimentation and 2) travel to field-campaign sites to collect and characterize atmospheric aerosols, and to analytical facilities for offline analysis of aerosol samples.
  • Modeling of aerosol phenomena. A candidate for this position should have an interest in simulating aerosol dynamics and phase-equilibria using continuum and particle methods. This position will involve development and testing of free-energy minimization approaches, kinetic/dynamic models, and computational chemistry methods (e.g., molecular dynamics) to simulate aerosol formation and properties (e.g., hygroscopicity, volatility, optical properties) of relevance for ultimately assessing health and climate impacts.

Please contact Satoshi Takahama <> regarding these positions, or for general inquiries regarding a Master's project or research (Ph.D. or postdoctoral) position in mathematical/numerical modeling, instrument development, field/laboratory experimentation, and/or statistical data analysis addressing topics relevant for atmospheric particle research, which may include

  • characterization of ambient and laboratory-generated, atmospherically relevant aerosol morphology and chemical composition
  • source apportionment of atmospheric aerosols
  • measurement and modeling of aerosol properties
