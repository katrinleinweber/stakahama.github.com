Residence permit

  • Lausanne commune. Take with you:
    • a completed worksheet/form (can complete there, but it's in French) which lists basic information (birthdate, country of origin, job, current address, etc.)
    • work permit from EPFL
    • proof of residence in Switzerland, or mailing address
    • photocopy of passport
    • birth certificate translated into French or English.
    • approximately 147 or 180 CHF, I forget which (147 CHF)

Health care and health insurance

  • Dental: Adent, especially Ecublens office has worked out well (Lausanne's also, but English-speaking dentist was harder to schedule, in my experience).
  • Doctor: haven't found yet.

Health insurance:

  • Insurance is not provided by employer. I have Assura, but they do not appear to provide an English website.
  • Accident insurance is provided for EPFL (apparently also covers personal accidents)
  • Dental and vision insurance is apparently not common in Switzerland as it is in the U.S. (though possible). However, dental costs are not as high as in the U.S.

Banking

  • Post office (Post Finance) will let you set up an account with ID and work contract.
  • BCV, UBS, CS may also allow this as well.
  • To get a credit card, need work permit. BCV: at least 3 months of deposit in their account. Credit Suisse: 3 pay stubs.
