Basic commands

C here denotes the <control> key, and M denotes the meta key (either <alt> or <escape>).

taskkeybindingfunction

saveC-x C-s(save-buffer)
save asC-x C-w(write-file)
escape/stopC-g(keyboard-quit)
undoC-x u(undo)
copyM-w(kill-ring-save)
pasteC-y(yank)
read-only (toggle)C-x C-q(toggle-read-only)
exitC-x C-c(save-buffers-kill-terminal)
  • Arrow keys also often work for navigation.
  • <shift> + arrow keys also work for highlighting, but you can also highlight regions by pressing C-<space> before and after cursor movement.

Navigation

Basic cursor movement:

unit of incrementbackwardforward
characterC-b (backward-char)C-f (forward-char)
wordM-b (backward-word)M-f (forward-word)
sentenceM-a (backward-sentence)M-e (forward-sentence)
lineC-a (beginning-of-line)C-e (end-of-line)
lineC-p (previous-line)C-n (next-line)
paragraphM-{ (backward-paragraph)M-} (next-paragraph)
pageM-v (scroll-up)C-v (scroll-down)
bufferM-< (beginning-of-buffer)M-> (end-of-buffer)
  • Prefix keystrokes with C-u n where n = number of times to repeat. For instance, to go up 10 lines rather than one: C-u 10 C-p.
  • M-a and M-e in shell mode and minibuffer take you to the point after the shell prompt rather than the line.
  • M-r moves cursor to left margin on center line of page (move-to-window-line); useful after scrolling (M-v or C-v).

Goto functions:

keybindingfunction
M-x goto-char(goto-char)
M-g g(goto-line)

Move to functions:

keybindingfunction
M-r(move-to-window-line)

Another way to move around the buffer is through incremental searching:

keybindingfunction
C-s(isearch-forward)
C-r(isearch-backward)
C-u C-s(isearch-regexp-forward)
C-u C-r(isearch-regexp-backward)

Reposition window relative to cursor:

keybindingfunction
C-l(recenter-top-bottom)
C-M-l(command-reposition-window)

Killing:

keybindingbackwardforward
characterDEL (delete-backward-char)C-d (delete-char)
wordM-DEL (backward-kill-word)M-d (kill-word)
lineC-k (kill-line)
sentenceC-x DEL (backward-kill-sentence)M-k (kill-sentence)
There are additional functions, (backward-kill-paragraph) and (kill-paragraph), which can be accessed interactively, but are not bound to keybindings by default. See also rectangle functions for killing and yanking.

Windows, frames, screens

(elscreen is a package installed separately.)

Management

functionwindowframeelscreen

kill-buffer and delete window/screenC-x 4 0C-z M-k
delete othersC-x 4 1C-x 5 1C-z K
otherC-x oC-x 5 oC-z p, C-z n
  • C-x o takes a prefix argument of COUNT, which is the number of windows to move forward/backward (-n to n).
  • I have mapped C-c o for switching among frames; C-u C-c o will move through frames in the opposite direction (akin to Command-` and Shift-Command-` or Alt-` and Shift-Alt-` allowed by the OS).
